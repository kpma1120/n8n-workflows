Introduction
Welcome to Busy Bee Wealth Management! We are excited to have you join our team and look forward to working with you to achieve our shared goals.
This handbook is designed to provide you with a general understanding of our policies, procedures, and benefits. Please keep in mind that it is not a contract, and the company reserves the right to modify any of the policies at any time. If you have any questions or concerns about the policies, please don't hesitate to ask your supervisor or a member of the HR department.
Purpose of the Handbook
The purpose of this handbook is to provide you with a general understanding of the company's policies, procedures, and benefits. It is not intended to be an exhaustive list of all policies or procedures, nor is it intended to replace any employment agreement or other agreement between you and the company.
Applicability of Policies
The policies in this handbook apply to all employees of Busy Bee Wealth Management, regardless of their position, tenure, or status. Some policies may not apply to certain categories of employees, such as executive employees, independent contractors, or temporary workers. If you have any questions about whether a particular policy applies to you, please ask your supervisor or a member of the HR department.
Employment at Will
Your employment with Busy Bee Wealth Management is at will, which means that either you or the company can terminate the employment relationship at any time and for any reason (except for an illegal reason, such as discrimination based on a protected characteristic). This policy can only be modified in writing and signed by an authorized representative of the company.
Review and Updates
This handbook is subject to change, and the company reserves the right to modify or eliminate any of the policies at any time. We encourage you to periodically review the handbook for updates. If you have any questions or concerns about changes to the policies, please speak with your supervisor or a member of the HR department.
Acknowledgment of Receipt
In order to acknowledge that you have received and reviewed this handbook, please sign the acknowledgment form provided and return it to the HR department. If you have any questions about the acknowledgment form, please ask a member of the HR department.
Anti-Retaliation
Busy Bee Wealth Management is committed to enforcing its policies in a fair and non-discriminatory manner. We will not tolerate retaliation against employees who raise concerns about policy violations or engage in protected activities, such as participating in an investigation or filing a complaint. If you believe that you have been retaliated against, please report it to your supervisor or a member of the HR department immediately.
Equal Employment Opportunity
Busy Bee Wealth Management is an equal opportunity employer and is committed to providing equal employment opportunities to all applicants and employees without regard to race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, or any other characteristic protected by federal, state, or local law. We will not tolerate discrimination or harassment of any kind.
Open Door Policy
We encourage you to communicate openly with your supervisors and the HR department about any concerns you may have regarding the company or its policies. Your input is essential to our ability to maintain a positive and productive workplace culture. If you have any suggestions, concerns, or complaints, please bring them to the attention of your supervisor or a member of the HR department.
Communication
We believe that effective communication is essential to our success. Our communication policies and procedures are designed to ensure that all employees are informed about the company's operations, policies, and procedures. Employees are expected to communicate in a professional and respectful manner at all times.
Confidentiality
Busy Bee Wealth Management values its confidential information and expects all employees to maintain the confidentiality of company information and to comply with any applicable confidentiality agreements they may have signed. This includes, but is not limited to, proprietary information, financial data, and personal employee information.
Code of Conduct
Busy Bee Wealth Management is committed to ethical conduct and to complying with all applicable laws and regulations. We expect our employees to act with integrity, honesty, and respect for others at all times. If you have any concerns about a violation of the company's policies or code of conduct, please report it to your supervisor or a member of the HR department immediately.

2. Equal Employment Opportunity
At Busy Bee Wealth Management, we are committed to creating and maintaining a workplace that is free from discrimination, harassment, and retaliation. It is our policy to provide equal employment opportunities to all applicants and employees without regard to their race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, sexual orientation, gender identity, veteran status, or any other characteristic protected by federal, state, or local law.
2.1 Discrimination
We will not tolerate discrimination in any form. This includes discrimination in any aspect of employment, such as hiring, promotion, salary, benefits, training, or termination. Discrimination can take many forms, including disparate treatment, disparate impact, and harassment. It is our policy to take prompt and effective action to prevent and correct any form of discrimination.
2.2 Harassment
Harassment is a form of discrimination that is prohibited under our policy and under federal, state, and local law. Harassment includes any conduct that creates an intimidating, hostile, or abusive work environment based on an individual's protected characteristic. Harassment can take many forms, including verbal or physical conduct, such as jokes, slurs, epithets, threats, intimidation, or other conduct that creates a hostile work environment.
Sexual harassment is a form of harassment that is prohibited by law. Sexual harassment includes any unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature when:
* Submission to such conduct is made either explicitly or implicitly a term or condition of an individual's employment,
* Submission to or rejection of such conduct by an individual is used as the basis for employment decisions affecting such individual, or
* Such conduct has the purpose or effect of unreasonably interfering with an individual's work performance or creating an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment.
2.3 Retaliation
Retaliation is any adverse action taken against an employee because he or she has engaged in a protected activity, such as filing a complaint of discrimination or harassment, participating in an investigation of such a complaint, or opposing any unlawful discriminatory practice. Retaliation is prohibited under our policy and under federal, state, and local law.
2.4 Reporting Discrimination, Harassment, or Retaliation
If you believe that you have been subjected to discrimination, harassment, or retaliation, or if you have witnessed any such conduct, we encourage you to report it immediately. You can report such conduct to your supervisor, the HR department, or any other manager or executive. Your report will be handled promptly, confidentially, and without retaliation.
2.5 Investigation of Complaints
We will investigate all reports of discrimination, harassment, or retaliation promptly, thoroughly, and impartially. We will take appropriate corrective action if we find that a violation of our policy has occurred.
2.6 Confidentiality
We will make every effort to maintain the confidentiality of all participants involved in an investigation of a discrimination, harassment, or retaliation complaint. However, we cannot guarantee complete confidentiality in all circumstances, as we may need to disclose information to conduct a thorough investigation, take appropriate corrective action, or comply with legal requirements.
2.7 No Retaliation
We will not tolerate any form of retaliation against anyone who reports discrimination, harassment, or retaliation, participates in an investigation of such a complaint, or opposes any unlawful discriminatory practice. Retaliation is a serious violation of our policy and will be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
2.8 Accommodations for Individuals with Disabilities
We are committed to making reasonable accommodations for individuals with disabilities, in accordance with applicable law. If you are an applicant or employee with a disability and require a reasonable accommodation to perform the essential functions of your job, please inform your supervisor or the HR department. We will engage in an interactive process with you to determine the appropriate accommodation.
2.9 Accommodations for Religious Beliefs
We are committed to accommodating the religious beliefs and practices of our applicants and employees, in accordance with applicable law. If you require an accommodation for a religious belief or practice, please inform your supervisor or the HR department. We will engage in an interactive process with you to determine the appropriate accommodation.
2.10 Affirmative Action
We are an equal opportunity employer and are committed to taking affirmative action to employ and advance in employment individuals without regard to their race, color, religion, sex, national origin, protected veteran status, or disability. We will make reasonable accommodations for qualified individuals with known disabilities unless doing so would result in an undue hardship.
2.11 Policy Dissemination and Training
We will disseminate this policy to all employees and applicants and provide training on equal employment opportunity and non-discrimination to all employees and managers.

3. Employee Conduct and Work Rules
At Busy Bee Wealth Management, we expect our employees to adhere to a high standard of conduct and professionalism. Our work rules are designed to ensure that our workplace is productive, safe, and respectful for all employees.
3.1 Compliance with Laws and Policies
All employees are expected to comply with all federal, state, and local laws, as well as our company policies and procedures. Violations of laws or policies may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
3.2 Professional Conduct
We expect all employees to act in a professional and ethical manner at all times. This includes treating all colleagues, clients, and vendors with respect and courtesy, and avoiding behavior that could harm the company's reputation or relationships.
3.3 Dress Code
Our dress code is professional business attire. Employees are expected to dress in a manner that is appropriate for their job duties and the work environment. Clothing must be clean, neat, and in good repair. If you are unsure about what is appropriate, please speak with your supervisor or the HR department.
3.4 Use of Company Property
Employees are expected to use company property, including computers, phones, and other equipment, for legitimate business purposes only. Personal use of company property is permitted only if it does not interfere with work performance or violate company policies. Employees are responsible for safeguarding company property and reporting any damage or theft immediately.
3.5 Internet and Email Use
Our internet and email systems are intended for business use only. Employees are prohibited from accessing or distributing inappropriate or offensive materials, including pornography, hate speech, and other forms of harassment. Employees should also avoid excessive personal use of the internet or email, as it can interfere with work performance and productivity.
3.6 Conflict of Interest
Employees are expected to avoid any situation that may create a conflict of interest between their personal or financial interests and the interests of the company. If you are unsure whether a situation constitutes a conflict of interest, please speak with your supervisor or the HR department.
3.7 Reporting of Misconduct
If you become aware of any misconduct, including violations of laws, regulations, or company policies, you are encouraged to report it immediately. You may report misconduct to your supervisor, the HR department, or through the company's anonymous reporting hotline. Retaliation against employees who report misconduct is strictly prohibited.
3.8 Drugs and Alcohol
We maintain a drug-free workplace and prohibit the use, possession, sale, or distribution of illegal drugs, alcohol, or controlled substances in the workplace. Employees who are impaired by drugs or alcohol are not allowed to work and may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
3.9 Sexual and Other Harassment
We are committed to providing a work environment that is free from harassment, including sexual harassment. Harassment of any kind, including verbal or physical conduct that creates a hostile or offensive work environment, is not tolerated.
3.10 Discrimination
We are committed to providing equal employment opportunities to all employees and applicants for employment without regard to race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, or any other characteristic protected by law.
3.11 Violence and Threats of Violence
We will not tolerate any form of violence or threats of violence in the workplace. This includes any behavior that is intended to intimidate, harass, or harm another person.
3.12 Solicitation and Distribution
Employees are prohibited from soliciting or distributing literature, merchandise, or other materials in the workplace without prior approval from management. This includes soliciting donations, selling products, or distributing political or religious materials.
